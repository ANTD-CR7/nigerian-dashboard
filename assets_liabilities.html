Key Insight

The CBN balance sheet expanded from approximately ₦3.5T in early 2005 to roughly ₦81T by late 2023 — peaking near ₦93T in October 2023 — driven by oil revenue growth, monetary policy operations and FX interventions. Because a balance sheet balances, total assets and total liabilities are equal and their lines overlap. Gold holdings represent Nigeria's international gold reserve position.

The CBN balance sheet — ₦3.5T to ₦93T

Total assets equal total liabilities — a balance sheet always balances — so one line represents both. The near-vertical jump in mid-2023 is real: the Naira devaluation revalued the CBN's foreign-currency assets, alongside the securitisation of the government's Ways & Means overdraft.
