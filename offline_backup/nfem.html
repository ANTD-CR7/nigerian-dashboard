What happened

The NFEM opened in December 2024 as Nigeria's reformed interbank FX market. Across 348 daily sessions to April 2026 the closing rate has ranged between about ₦1,337 and ₦1,660/$, set by live market supply and demand — easing to roughly ₦1,374/$ by the end of the window.

Why it matters

Unlike the CBN's administrative rate, NFEM rates come from actual trades between authorised dealers — so they're the closest market read on what the Naira is really worth from one day to the next.

How to read it

The blue line is the daily closing rate; green and red are the day's highest and lowest, and gold is the weighted average. A wider gap between high and low means a more volatile trading day.
