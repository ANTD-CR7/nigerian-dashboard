Monetary Policy Rate

CBN benchmark interest rate at each MPC decision — Central Bank of Nigeria · 2020–2026

Loading…
What happened

The CBN held the MPR at 11.5% through the COVID period, then raised it aggressively from 2022 — reaching 27.5% by 2024, a cumulative 1,600 basis-point tightening — to fight the post-reform inflation surge.

Why it matters

The MPR is the CBN's benchmark interest rate. Raising it makes borrowing more expensive across the whole economy, which is the main tool a central bank uses to slow inflation down.

How to read it

The line is stepped because the rate only changes at MPC meetings and holds flat in between. Each step up is a tightening decision; a plateau signals the CBN holding as it assesses inflation.
